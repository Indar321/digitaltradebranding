05 — Tone & Voice

BRAND
VOICE

The brand speaks with authority, never arrogance. It is direct without being cold, opinionated without being preachy, and minimal without being sterile.

In this section
Voice Pillars Writing Style Example Copy
◼
DIRECT

No filler words. No passive voice. Say exactly what needs to be said in as few words as possible. Brevity signals respect for the reader's intelligence.

"We design systems. Not decoration."
◼
BOLD

Take positions. Have opinions. The brand never hedges with "perhaps" or "might." We know what we think — and we say it plainly.

"Most design is timid. Ours isn't."
◼
PRECISE

Every word is chosen deliberately. Specificity builds credibility. Avoid vague terms like "innovative," "cutting-edge," or "world-class."

"6px border radius. Not 5. Not 8. 6."
◼
DRY

Humor exists — but it's earned through understatement, not performance. The brand doesn't try to be funny. It just is, occasionally.

"We accept compliments. And projects."
WRITING RULES

Headlines: Bebas Neue. Uppercase. No punctuation except em-dash (—). Body: Short sentences. Under 20 words preferred. Avoid adverbs. Never use "very," "really," or "extremely." Numbers: Always numerals, never spelled out. Dates: DD.MM.YYYY format only.

COMPONENT LAWS

Border-radius: always 0. Border-width: always 2px (interactive) or 3px (structural). No box-shadow anywhere. No gradient fills. All interactive states use color inversion (cream↔black) or red on hover. Transition duration: 150ms only. Easing: ease (never ease-in-out for fast interactions).

Patterns may only appear at opacity ≤ 12% when used as overlays. Never stack two patterns on the same surface. The noise texture (always applied at opacity 0.03–0.06) may be used on any solid background to add warmth and tactility — it is the only texture applied globally.

08 — Motion Language

MOTION
RULES

Motion in this identity is purposeful, never decorative. It communicates state change, draws attention to priority content, and rewards attention.

In this section

DURATION: 150ms / 300ms / 600ms
EASING: ease / cubic-bezier(0.16,1,0.3,1)
DELAY: 0ms / 100ms steps only
NEVER: bounce / elastic / spring
RESPECT: prefers-reduced-motion
MOTION PRINCIPLES

Fast in, slow out. Entrances are quick (150–300ms), exits even faster (100–200ms). Scroll reveals use 600ms with 24px translateY. Page transitions: fade only. No slide, no zoom, no bounce. Never animate more than 2 elements simultaneously on the same viewport. Respect prefers-reduced-motion — all animations must be disabled when this is set.

09 — Photography Rules

IMAGE
STYLE

Photography in this brand is architectural, raw, and deliberate. High contrast. Strong shadow. Minimal subject matter. Always shot against neutral backgrounds.

In this section
Style Rules Treatment Don'ts
◼
HIGH CONTRAST

Prefer images with strong tonal contrast. Avoid flat, low-contrast photography. The brand palette has no mid-tones — images should reflect this.

◼
DESATURATED

All brand photography is desaturated 60–80%. Never full color. The warmth of the identity comes from the cream palette, not from photo color.

▲
ARCHITECTURAL FRAMING

Strong geometric composition. Rule of thirds ignored in favor of centered, symmetric, or dramatically off-center framing. Horizon lines must be level.

▲
MINIMAL SUBJECT

One clear subject per image. No busy backgrounds. No crowds. No lifestyle stock photography. Real spaces, real materials, real work — nothing staged.

✓ APPROVED PHOTOGRAPHY
✓Architecture & interior shots with strong geometry
✓Studio process shots — tools, hands, materials
✓Product flat-lays on cream or black backgrounds
✓Portrait shots with dark or neutral backgrounds, no fill flash
✕ NEVER USE
✕Stock photography from Shutterstock/iStock/Getty
✕Heavily filtered or Instagram-style edited images
✕Full-color bright photography
✕Lifestyle or "team laughing at laptop" imagery
